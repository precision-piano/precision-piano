Most home pianos benefit from tuning twice a year, once as the heat comes on in the fall and once as it goes off in the spring. Those are the two points where humidity swings the most, so they're also when a piano is most likely to have drifted from pitch.

Pianos that get heavy use — a church piano played every week, a teaching studio's instrument, or a piano in a performance hall — often need more frequent attention, sometimes every few months, to stay reliable for performance.

## A few signs it's time

- Notes sound noticeably different from each other in pitch
- Chords sound "beating" or slightly dissonant
- It's been more than six months since the last tuning
- The piano has recently been moved
